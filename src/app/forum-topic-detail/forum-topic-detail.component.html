Hi Michael! I made this exact transition about 3 years ago. I was also in ML research at the university before moving to industry.

The biggest difference I noticed was the focus on practical applications and business impact. In academia, we're often focused on novel approaches, but in industry, the emphasis is on reliable, scalable solutions that solve real business problems.

My advice:

  1. Build a portfolio - Take on some practical projects that demonstrate you can solve real-world problems
  2. Practice coding interviews - LeetCode and HackerRank were invaluable for me
  3. Network with alumni - Many of us have made this transition and can refer you
  4. Highlight your research skills - The ability to systematically approach complex problems is highly valued

Feel free to DM me if you'd like to chat more specifically about your situation!

Great advice from Sarah! I'll add a couple more points from my experience hiring people making this transition:

Communication skills - Being able to explain complex technical concepts to non-technical stakeholders is crucial in industry. Practice this!

Understand the business context - Research the companies you're applying to and understand their business model, customers, and challenges.

Your research background is actually a strength - it shows you can work independently and tackle complex problems. Just make sure to frame it in terms of business impact during interviews.
